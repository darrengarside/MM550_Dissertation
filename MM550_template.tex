\subsection{Initial search on CWB, AI and network on subPrimo}
\subsubsection{refs:\#1-29}
The earliest mentions of `anchor institutions' are in the context of connecting broadband fibre networks in rural USA. Although not related to Community Wealth Building (CWB) or Anchor Institutions (AnI) as explored in this dissertation, this usage is still relevant when considering network connectivity, walks and structures.

I initially read abstracts and created a number of codes to categorise the articles. AnI articles were mostly subdivided into institution type e.g. AnI:Universities and whereas CWB related more to activities e.g. CWB:EconomicDevelopment.

CWB appears to date from early 2000s and I wonder if this is an offshoot of the Jubilee2000 debt-relief campaign. One article makes clear links between CWB and net-zero. Ii might be useful to clarify whether/how net-zero is linked to UN SDGs.

Community Wealth Act (signed into law March 2026) is a strategically enabling act injuncting various duties on Ministers of Scottish Government and requiring all 32 local authorities to produce and monitor action plans. No parto fthe Act referes to Anchor Institutions but there is a schedule that enumerates some nationally significant bodies.  Implications of this is that CWB is context for understanding AnIs and it is the latter that is the focus of the dissertation.
